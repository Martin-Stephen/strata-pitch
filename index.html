02 · Problem

Three stakeholders, one broken middle

STRATA
02 / PROBLEM
The problem

Everyone in private credit is losing money to the missing middle.

Retail wants yield but can't access it. Credit managers want retail AUM but can't reach it compliantly. Platforms want alts product but can't build the rails. Nobody in AU/NZ has closed the gap.

— Retail investor

Locked out of the best yield.

~4% term deposits vs ~6–8% private credit (indicative, historical). Today the answer is either an A$500K wholesale minimum, a clunky self-managed super structure, or an ASX listed trust with thin UX. No $100 mobile-native access. Squirrel has a waiting list.

— Credit manager

Can't reach retail without breaking themselves.

8 AU mid-tier managers (A$100M–A$2B AUM) have the product and want retail inflows. Building compliant retail distribution takes 18+ months, A$1M+ legal, and full-time platform staff. ASIC's REP 820 (the Nov 2025 private-credit surveillance report) just raised the bar higher.

— Channel / wealth platform

Demands alt product they can't source.

HUB24 and Praemium have grown alternatives FUA 20–25% p.a. for three years. Advisers are asking for fractional private credit. Nobody in AU/NZ has built the rails to supply it. They're buying partners (HUB24/Reach) to fill the gap.

10 · Economics

Revenue as AUA grows

STRATA
10 / ECONOMICS
Rails economics

Four revenue streams per issuer. Zero credit, asset or origination risk. Lower clip, lower risk, compounding scale — the Stripe pattern.

01 · Setup
$25–100k
One-off integration fee per issuer. Sub-trust spun up, tokens minted, channel mapped.
02 · SaaS
$2–10k/mo
Platform licence per issuer. Dashboards, reporting, ledger, compliance, support.
03 · AUA fee
30–95 bps
Annual on administered assets (bps = basis points, hundredths of a percent). Revenue tied to AUA, not user behaviour. Compounds.
04 · Secondary
15–40 bps
Transaction fee on each secondary-market trade. Pure marginal margin.

Worked example · 5 issuers × $100M AUA × 60 bps + setup + SaaS = ~$3.5M ARR at steady state. 10 issuers × $250M = ~$17M ARR.

15 · Risk

The objections, pre-empted

15 / RISK
Risk & mitigation

Five honest objections. Five sharp answers.

Objection How we mitigate
Pre-revenue · no users Correct. Regulator, licence and platform risk already converted to zero. Seed funds the only remaining conversion — first 2 live funds + 1k retail cohort in 9 months.
Pivoting from property The architecture was built and regulated for a fractional-property use-case; nothing was ever run in production. Private credit is the commercial launch on the same rails — pure debt, cleaner operational economics, no property management or vacancy risk, and a materially larger retail-distribution gap to fill.
NZ regulatory risk post-Du Val REP 820 in AU and post-Du Val posture in NZ are tailwinds for compliant rails, not headwinds. Strata is the credible rails — that's the whole point. At sandbox expiry (Nov 2027), the credit workflow maps cleanly onto a standard MIS Manager licence application.
Distribution concentration risk The direct Strata retail front-end is the day-one rail — we don't depend on any single third-party channel to reach retail. Sharesies is active in parallel; additional NZ platforms (Snowball, Kernel, InvestNow) are available for expansion. An ASX LIT feeder serves as the AU-retail shortcut while the AU MIT structure is stood up.
Execution risk on first issuer Research-backed timeline: 4–6 months from NDA to live fund with mid-tier manager. 8-name target list (Revolution AM, Rixon, Zagga, Pallas, Merricks, Pengana, PCG, First AM). Probability-weighted, one close from eight conversations is very achievable.
